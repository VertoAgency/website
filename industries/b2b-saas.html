What's different

B2B SaaS doesn't sell like everything else. The marketing has to account for that.

B2B SaaS marketing underperforms for a few structural reasons — and most agencies, trained on lead-gen heuristics, miss all of them.

The measurement gap · why SaaS paid budgets underperform

Recurring revenue redefines the win.

A signed logo isn't the win — retention and expansion are. Marketing that optimizes for first-touch conversion and stops there is optimizing for the wrong number. The work has to connect to net revenue retention, not just new-logo pipeline.

The buyer is a committee, not a persona.

A champion, an economic buyer, a technical evaluator, a procurement gate — sometimes a security or compliance reviewer. The deal closes when enough of them align. Reaching "the buyer" as if they were one person is why most B2B SaaS campaigns stall between MQL and opportunity.

The motion is rarely pure.

Most B2B SaaS sits between self-serve and enterprise sales. Product signups and sales-led pipeline run in parallel. Marketing has to feed both — and tie product-usage signal back to the campaigns that drove it.

The ad platforms can't see what closes.

Form fills happen in the platform's view. Deals close weeks later in the CRM. Without a data layer connecting the two, the bid algorithms optimize for whoever fills a form — not whoever buys. This is the single most common reason SaaS paid budgets underperform.

The buying committee

The deal closes when the committee aligns — not when one person says yes.

Each role has different concerns, engages on different channels, and responds to different content. Marketing's job isn't reaching the champion — it's giving each member of the committee what they need to say yes.

Economic Buyer · Fund approver

CMO, VP Marketing, or CFO. Responds to pipeline impact, peer-validated ROI stories, and board-level outcomes. Not persuaded by features.

LinkedIn AI targeting · ABM · case study content

Technical Evaluator · Integration reviewer

Engineering lead, solutions architect, or IT director. Validates the integration story, API depth, and support quality. Responds to documentation and technical content.

Paid search · technical SEO · doc-led content

Security / Compliance Reviewer · Risk gatekeeper

Relevant in regulated verticals and larger enterprises. Evaluates trust posture, data handling, and compliance certifications. Can block a deal in the final stage.

Specialized content · late-cycle ABM

Procurement Gate · Commercial gatekeeper

Validates pricing benchmarks, negotiates terms, and controls the timeline to signature. Responds to ROI proof and multi-year value modeling. Arrives late — but can stall deals indefinitely.

Sales enablement · ROI modeling · contact-level ABM
