What's different

B2B SaaS doesn't sell like everything else. The marketing has to account for that.

B2B SaaS marketing underperforms for a few structural reasons — and most agencies, trained on lead-gen heuristics, miss all of them. Four dynamics shape every program that works in this category.

01

Recurring revenue redefines the win.

A signed logo isn't the win — retention and expansion are. Marketing that optimizes for first-touch conversion and stops there is optimizing for the wrong number. The work has to connect to net revenue retention, not just new-logo pipeline.

02

The buyer is a committee, not a persona.

A champion, an economic buyer, a technical evaluator, a procurement gate — sometimes a security or compliance reviewer. The deal closes when enough of them align. Reaching "the buyer" as if they were one person is why most B2B SaaS campaigns stall between MQL and opportunity.

03

The motion is rarely pure.

Most B2B SaaS sits between self-serve and enterprise sales. Product signups and sales-led pipeline run in parallel, and the marketing has to feed both — and tie product-usage signal back to the campaigns that drove it.

04

The ad platforms can't see what closes.

Form fills happen in the platform's view. Deals close weeks later in the CRM. Without a data layer connecting the two, the bid algorithms optimize for whoever fills a form — not whoever buys. This is the single most common reason SaaS paid budgets underperform.

The buying committee

The deal closes when the committee aligns — not when one person says yes.

Across every B2B SaaS category, more than one person has to say yes. The exact committee shifts by vertical — a security architect gates a cyber deal, a Head of Compliance gates a fintech deal, a data-leadership reviewer gates an infrastructure deal — but the structure is constant: a champion who wants it, an economic buyer who funds it, a technical evaluator who validates it, and a procurement gate that closes it.

Marketing's job isn't to reach the champion. It's to give each member of the committee what they need to say yes — the right content, on the right channel, sequenced across a cycle that often runs six to eighteen months. Running campaigns "to the buyer" as if they were one person is why pipeline stalls.

What the committee looks like — and what moves it — depends on your category. We've mapped it in detail for the three we work in most.
